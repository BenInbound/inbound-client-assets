Week-by-Week Timeline

This timeline maps the recommended scope to a roughly 10-week delivery schedule. Strategy and design run first to lock in direction before any development begins. Content writing runs in parallel with design and development so finished copy is ready when templates are built. Weeks marked as milestones include client review points.

Timeline assumes reasonable client response times (2-3 business days for feedback). Delays in approvals will shift subsequent phases.

1
Week 1 Strategy & Kickoff
  • Project kickoff and setup (tools, access, Slack channel)Ben
  • Strategy workshop with client incl. prep (4h, 3 people)Ben + Text writer + Martin
  • Document strategy outcomes: goals, audiences, prioritiesBen
  • Technical review of current HubSpot setup (modules, templates)Ben

Outcome: Agreed priorities, confirmed page list for full rewrites vs. light edits, member page restyle confirmed.

2
Week 2 Sitemap & Style Direction
  • New sitemap and page structure in FigJamBen
  • Homepage mockup / style direction in FigmaBen
  • Content strategy for priority pages (define briefs)Text writer

Outcome: Sitemap approved, visual direction agreed based on homepage concept.

✓
Milestone Client Approval: Strategy + Style Direction

Client reviews and approves the sitemap, page structure, and homepage visual direction. This locks in the design system foundation. Content writing begins based on the confirmed page list from the strategy workshop.

3-4
Weeks 3-4 Design System & Template Design
  • Full design system in Figma (typography, colors, components, spacing, buttons, forms)Ben
  • Wireframe + visual design: service/product page templateBen
  • Wireframe + visual design: blog listing + post templateBen
  • Wireframe + visual design: contact/about templateBen
  • Begin content writing: homepage + top 2-3 priority pagesText writer

Design and content run in parallel. Text writer works from agreed briefs while templates are designed.

✓
Milestone Client Review: Template Designs

Client reviews all 5 template designs in Figma. One round of revisions included. After sign-off, development begins.

5
Week 5 Design Revisions + Dev Kickoff
  • Design revisions from client feedback (1 round)Ben
  • Begin HubSpot development: design system CSS + global header/footerBen (AI-assisted)
  • Continue content writing: remaining priority pagesText writer
6-7
Weeks 6-7 Core Development
  • Build homepage template in HubSpotBen (AI-assisted)
  • Build service/product page templateBen (AI-assisted)
  • Build contact/about templateBen (AI-assisted)
  • Build blog listing + post templateBen (AI-assisted)
  • Form implementations (3-4 forms)Ben (AI-assisted)
  • Light content edits on secondary pagesText writer

Content is populated into templates as both become ready. Member page template restyle is included in this phase.

8
Week 8 Blog Migration & SEO
  • Bulk blog template migration (287 posts)Ben
  • Blog category structure implementationBen
  • URL mapping for changed pagesBen
  • 301 redirect planBen
  • Meta descriptions for priority pagesText writer

Blog posts may need some manual cleanup where formatting breaks in the new template. Flag these for the client.

9
Week 9 QA & Testing
  • Full template QA across breakpoints (7 templates, all devices)Ben
  • Form testing (submissions, notifications, CRM sync)Ben
  • Member page template spot checksBen
  • Bug fixesBen
  • Content QA: proofread all new and edited pagesText writer
✓
Milestone Client Review: Staging Site

Client reviews the complete staging site. All templates, content, blog migration, and redirects are in place. Final feedback collected before launch.

10
Week 10 Launch & Handover
  • Final bug fixes from staging reviewBen
  • Activate redirects and go liveBen
  • Post-launch smoke test (all templates, forms, key pages)Ben
  • Client training: HubSpot CMS editing, blog publishing, HubDB (if applicable)Ben

Site is live. Any blog posts needing manual formatting cleanup are flagged for the client to handle at their own pace.

+
Buffer Contingency

The 15% buffer built into the hour estimate covers unexpected complexity, additional bug fixes, or minor scope adjustments discovered during development. If client feedback cycles take longer than 2-3 business days, the timeline may extend by 1-2 weeks.
